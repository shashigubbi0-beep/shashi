A waterfall is a natural feature where a river or stream flows over a steep, rocky edge, creating a vertical descent of water. It is a stunning sight, often found in mountain regions where water cascades into a pool below, surrounded by misty, scenic landscapes. Here are several examples of sentences using the word "waterfall": Descriptive: The majestic waterfall cascaded down the rocky cliff, filling the air with a cool mist. Nature/Scenery: We hiked for hours through the dense forest until we reached the breathtaking waterfall. Simple: The waterfall is an impressive sight, dropping thirty meters into the pool below. Action: Visitors watched in awe as the river became a dramatic waterfall. Metaphorical: "For an instant, silence, noisier than a waterfall".
